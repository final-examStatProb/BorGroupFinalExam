Probability

Definition: Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to occur.

Example: A dice is rolled. What is the probability of getting a 6?

Solution: There is 1 favorable outcome (rolling a 6) out of 6 possible outcomes. Probability = 1/6 ≈ 16.67%.

Permutation

Definition: A permutation is an arrangement of objects in a specific order.

Example: In how many ways can 3 people (A, B, C) be seated in a row?

Solution: The number of arrangements is 3! = 3 × 2 × 1 = 6.

Combination

Definition: A combination refers to selecting items from a group where order does not matter.

Example: How many ways can you select 2 fruits from a basket of 3 (Apple, Banana, Cherry)?

Solution: The number of combinations is C(3, 2) = 3! / [(2!)(3-2)!] = 3.

Measures of Central Tendency (Ungrouped Data)

Definition: Measures of central tendency describe the central or typical value of a dataset.

Example: Consider the dataset: {2, 4, 6, 6, 8}.

  • Mean: (2 + 4 + 6 + 6 + 8) / 5 = 26 / 5 = 5.2
  • Median: The middle value is 6.
  • Mode: The most frequent value is 6.

Fundamental Counting Principle

Definition: The Fundamental Counting Principle is a method to determine the total number of outcomes.

Example: A menu has 3 appetizers and 2 drinks. How many meal combinations are possible?

Solution: Total combinations = 3 × 2 = 6.
